1. Core Concept

This economic model proposes a shift from commodity-backed currency to a Population-Backed Standard.

The value of currency is derived from the living human population. Every person backs $1,000,000 (or $10,000,000) of money supply. This funds a Universal Basic Income (UBI) distributed to all citizens, creating natural wealth convergence toward the asset value V.

Key Insight: The mathematical dynamics are scale-invariant. Whether P=1,000 or P=7,000,000,000, the convergence behavior, tax proportions, and equilibrium point remain identical. Only the absolute dollar magnitudes change.

8. Comprehensive Q&A

Q: Why does a billionaire pay the same tax amount regardless of population size?

A: Because Tax = W × (U/V). Population (P) cancels out of the formula. A billionaire with $1B wealth pays $1B × 0.002 = $2M/month in tax at U=$2K, whether the total population is 1,000 or 7 billion.

Q: Why does V=$10M have slower convergence?

A: Convergence speed depends on the ratio U/V. At V=$10M, U=$2K, the rate is 0.02%/month vs 0.2%/month at V=$1M. The half-life formula t½ = ln(0.5)/ln(1-r) shows that smaller r means longer half-life.

Q: Which V value should we choose?

A: It's a policy decision. V=$1M: faster equalization, lower savings floor. V=$10M: slower equalization, higher savings floor. Democratic governance could adjust V based on societal values and economic conditions.

Q: Does the equilibrium point change with U?

A: No. Equilibrium is always V. At W=V: Tax = V × (U/V) = U. UBI = U. Net = U - U = $0. The equilibrium is mathematically fixed regardless of U.

Q: What about inflation with higher U?

A: Higher U means more money circulation, but also faster wealth redistribution. The model prioritizes human security over strict price stability. If prices rise, everyone's UBI and wealth floor rise proportionally since V can be adjusted.

Q: Can parameters be changed over time?

A: Yes. Both U and V are tunable parameters. A society could start with lower U for stability, then increase it to accelerate equalization, or adjust V to change the savings floor. Changes would affect convergence speed but not the fundamental dynamics.
